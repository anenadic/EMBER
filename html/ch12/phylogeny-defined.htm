Phylogeny

Species groups are descended from a common ancestral species. In general, the genealogical connections take the form of a tree (a phylogenetic tree), representing the phylogeny of the group.

Phylogenetic analysis exploits distinguishing features between taxa (singular taxon) to calculate phylogenetic trees. Usually, measures of difference between sequence pairs in multiple alignments are used. Calculated trees contain branch points, and the distance between them reflects the evolutionary distance. Phylogeny thus endeavours to reconstruct the evolutionary history of genetic information, as observed in sequence alignments.
